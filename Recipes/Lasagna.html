Lasagna Recipe

Description

Lasagna is basically the ultimate comfort food in pasta form. It’s an Italian dish made by layering wide, flat pasta
sheets with rich sauces, creamy cheeses, and (traditionally) meats or vegetables. Think of it like a savory “layer
cake,” but instead of frosting, you’ve got tomato sauce, béchamel (white sauce) or ricotta, melted mozzarella, and
whatever fillings you love.

The word lasagna comes from the Greek word laganon (the world’s earliest form of pasta), but it really took shape in Italy,
especially Emilia-Romagna, where the classic version with ragù (meat sauce), béchamel, and Parmesan was born. Over time,
it spread worldwide, with tons of variations—meat-heavy, veggie-packed, dairy-free, gluten-free, you name it.
